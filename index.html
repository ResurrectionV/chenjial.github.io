About Me

I am a graduate student in Computer Science at Johns Hopkins University, building on my experiences at Boston University and Rutgers University, where I earned a triple major in Computer Science, Statistics, and Mathematics. Driven by both academic rigor and real-world applications, I’ve explored a wide range of topics in Sentiment Analysis, Deep Generative Model, Data Analysis techniques, and more.

My current research focuses on deep learning for video reasoning, segmentation, and more broadly, bridging theoretical insights with practical solutions in computer vision and language models. I’ve co-authored publications under Professor Mathias Unberath’s guidance at the Arcade Lab, developing digital twin-based segmentation approaches that increase both region-similarity and contour-accuracy metrics by over 30%, and I'm currently preparing a NeurIPS submission extending these techniques to tasks like VQA, Grounding, and summarization.

Prior to JHU, I contributed to the Art and AI Laboratory at Rutgers, delving into generative adversarial networks and stable diffusion fine-tuning. I enjoy investigating the reliability and interpretability of modern AI, especially in sequential data such as human language and time-series signals. Outside the lab, you might find me refining my programming skills (Python, Java, C, or assembly), sampling new ideas from classic literature, or wrestling with Kaggle’s AI challenges in my quest for continuous improvement.

In the future, I plan to push boundaries in computer vision, advanced machine learning, and real-time AI systems, leveraging my robust mathematical foundation and my knack for interdisciplinary research. I speak Mandarin, English, and Japanese, and I’m always excited to collaborate on ambitious projects that merge scientific rigor with creative insight. Feel free to review my resume and reach out for collaboration or discussion.

Looking ahead, I am actively seeking roles in LLM and MLE positions, and exploring PhD opportunities that combine strong theoretical underpinnings with tangible real-world impact. My robust mathematical foundation, cross-disciplinary mindset, and passion for research continue to drive me toward new innovations in AI.
